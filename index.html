About Me

Hi! I'm Nick, a Third Year studying Computer Science at the University of Virginia. I started my software development journey about half a year ago and haven't looked back since!

Throughout the past six months, I've gathered skills in creating terminal applications with Python and Java and the basics of web development with HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I've also had a bit of experience working with AWS tools and Data Engineering in my time as a research assistant at Data Driven Streets.

At the moment I'm focusing my energy on Track C of the Headstarter Fellowship (making an open source contribution) and Angela Yu's web development bootcamp! In the future, I want to learn more about applications of AI and ML.

In my free time, I like playing volleyball, going climbing, and trying new foods! I also enjoy playing videogames like League and Elden Ring.

Leadership

Chinese Student Association at UVA

May 2023 - Present
Roles : Culture Chair, Treasurer

The Chinese Student Association seeks to promote awareness and appreciation for traditional and modern Chinese culture within the UVA Community. The club hosts a variety of cultural and social events to reach out to and bring together all parts of the university community to share and celebrate Chinese Culture. As a past officer and now exec member, it is my duty to ensure that this mission comes to fruition.

As culture chair, my main responsibility was to plan the semesterly cultural events for CSA. This came in the form of logistical planning of over 100 people for a three hour show highlighting the Chinese cultural importance of benevolence through a video skit, traditional and modern dances, and chinese martial arts. Now, as a Treasurer, my responsibility has expanded to not only take care of the money of the club, but also manage a board of 20 people to ensure CSA continues to foster a welcoming community for its members.

Projects

Screen Stopper

July 2024

Technologies: HTML, CSS, JS

Screen Stopper is a chrome extension that pauses use of youtube shorts (and other social media apps in the future) and creates a popup to stop continuous social media use. My motivation behind this app came from a realization that I would constantly get distracted by youtube shorts while trying to concentrate on work. This caused me to become terribly inefficient with my working time and I wanted to put a stop to it.

Dog-Quiz

June 2024

Technologies: HTML, CSS, JS

A web game designed to test your knowledge of dogs on the internet! Though it was made through basic HTML, CSS, and Javascript, I was able to learn a lot about async Javascript and Bootstrap as well as implement basic API calls.

Puzzle Game

May 2024

Technologies: Unity, C#

This puzzle game was inspired by the swapping mechanics of the mobile game Puzzles & Dragons and the board game Rubik's Race. The game randomizes a 3x3 board with up to 5 different colors, in which the user must utilize P&D's swapping mechanic in order to generate the same 3x3 board in the center of their 5x5 board. This was my first attempt at making a game with Unity, and though it definitely wasn't the cleanest in terms of organization and efficiency, the desired functionality was achieved.
